Activities

Mining

Sapropel and peat deposits are found throughout the country, but the best are those that are far from cities and industrial centers. Such deposits are usually environmentally friendly, do not even contain traces of anthropogenic and technogenic impact. We mine in places that have passed preliminary research

Research results

The concentrate increases the number of microorganisms in the soil that decompose poorly soluble mineral and organic phosphorus compounds. After the application of the concentrate, the soil supply with assimilable nitrogen reserves improves: the number of ammonifying bacteria increases 3-5 times, in some cases a tenfold increase in ammonifiers was recorded; nitrifying bacteria - 3-7 times. Due to the improvement of the living conditions of free-living bacteria, when the concentrate is added, their ability to fix molecular nitrogen from the atmosphere increases almost 10 times

The introduction of the concentrate into the soil significantly intensifies the activity of various groups of microorganisms, which are closely related to the mobilization of soil nutrients and the transformation of potential fertility into effective

The introduction of humic fertilizers improves the physical, physicochemical properties of the soil, its air, water and thermal regime. Humic acids, together with mineral and organomineral soil particles, form a soil absorbing complex, which determines its absorption capacity

Molecular complexes of TSC are included in the soil structure; in their presence, the exchange capacity of soils sharply increases. The adsorbed forms of nutrients do not bind to the soil, are not washed out by water, and are available for use by plants. In the future, the plants use these adsorbed substances, and more intensively than from the soil solution

The introduction of TSC is especially important for lands with a low humus content during long-term monoculture. For soil zones with cold and temperate climates, the ability of the concentrate to color the soil in a dark color is essential. By forming the color, the concentrate changes its thermal regime. Cold clay soils get warmer.

The introduction of humic fertilizers into the soil stimulates the activity of microorganisms and promotes a more accelerated decomposition of pesticides in the soil. At the same time, the resistance of plants to the action of these chemicals increases, the rate of decomposition of poisons in the cells of the plant itself increases, and the negative effect of high doses of mineral fertilizers, especially nitrogen fertilizers, is removed
